PAST EVENTS

How To Start Programming

The "How To Start Programming" workshop initiated with the MTC core council introducing the club's goals, events, and opportunities. The speakers provided insights into programming levels, discussed what BITS Pilani offers, and shared information on competitive programming, open-source projects, along with a curated list of resources for participants to begin learning their preferred programming languages.

MATLAB Workshop

The “MATLAB Workshop '' began with the two speakers introducing the UI of MATLAB and its basic syntax and functionalities. They moved on to talking about the data types, comments, arrays, linspace, matrix element-wise operations, and indexing. Next, they solved two sample problems that aimed to demonstrate problem-solving and plotting graphs in MATLAB.

VS Code Workshop

The “VS Code” workshop began with the core council members of MTC, Heramb and Akash first introducing the agendas of the workshop and giving a brief intro to VS Code to the participants. The speakers then continued to discuss programming, the various uses of VS code, tips and tricks, and making it personalized to one’s liking. They shared information and ideas regarding API integration and various intricacies it offers such as built in AI integration, as well as a carefully curated list of resources that can help in familiarizing the VS Code environment.

Typing Contest

The Typing Contest, Microsoft Tech Club's flagship event, tested participants' auditory comprehension and typing speed in a Computer Lab. Participants had two hours to attempt the challenge, with the opportunity for two retries. Each hour featured four unique audio clips, and participants were evaluated on speed and accuracy. The competition had a total of 9 winners, with 3 winners from each hour.

SignQuest

The MTC SignQuest is a promotional event for the ESL Global Summit Generative AI Competition, being organized by MTC at the International Conference on Computational Intelligence and Network Systems (CINS 2023), which is being held at BITS Pilani Dubai Campus in hybrid mode. The competition was held at the vending machine area and allowed participants in pairs to take part. One in the duo had to answer the quiz using the searching skills of the second participant. The aim of the competition was to spread awareness regarding Emirati Sign Language, and of the Generative AI competition.

ThinkAI’23

ThinkAI’23 was a competition that attempted to scour for talented students with an idea for an AI-powered solution to the world’s problems so as to provide them with mentorship and guidance and prepare them for the 2024 Microsoft Imagine Cup. A total of 16 teams registered for the competition, and 14 teams presented their pitches to 8 different judges. The ideas ranged from using AI in the fields of sustainability, healthcare and food science to mental health, philanthropy and education, to name just a few. The winners of the competition were Team Tech Titans (Sanober Sarfaraz Ahmed and Harsh Garg) and Team IntelliTrack (Anurag Kumar Jha, Mohammed Husamuddin, Sstuti D Mehra, Abdul Rahmaan Ansari).

Speaker Session with Professor Nick Pears

The Microsoft Tech Club organized a speaker session with Professor Nick Pears, an esteemed expert in Computer Vision from the University of York. The event, attended by BITS Pilani Dubai Campus director Dr. Srinivasan Madapusi and Computer Science Department HoD Dr. Vijaykumar, featured insights into extensive research areas, including Artificial Intelligence and Cyber Security. Professor Pears highlighted his contributions to craniofacial shape modeling, fostering engaging discussions on Computer Vision's current state and future trends.
